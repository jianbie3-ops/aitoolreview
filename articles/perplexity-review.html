What Is Perplexity AI?

Perplexity AI is an AI-powered answer engine that combines large language models with real-time web search to deliver concise, cited answers to questions. Unlike traditional search engines that return a list of links, Perplexity reads, synthesizes, and summarizes information from across the web — then presents it with inline citations so you can verify every claim.

Founded in 2022 by Aravind Srinivas and backed by the likes of Jeff Bezos and Nvidia, Perplexity has evolved rapidly. In 2026, it supports multi-model access (letting you choose between GPT-5, Claude 4, Gemini 2.5, and its own fine-tuned models), file upload analysis, collections for organizing research, and Spaces for shared team workspaces.

For many users — researchers, students, analysts, and knowledge workers — Perplexity has already replaced Google as their primary search tool.

Key Features of Perplexity AI in 2026

1. Real-Time Search with Citations — The Killer Feature

This is Perplexity's signature capability. Ask any question — "What are the latest Q3 earnings for Nvidia?" or "Summarize the key arguments in the latest AI regulation bill" — and Perplexity delivers a synthesized answer with inline citations numbered directly in the response. Click any citation to open the source page.

Unlike ChatGPT or Claude's web search (which sometimes hallucinates sources or retrieves outdated information), Perplexity's search layer is purpose-built for accuracy. It actively crawls the web, evaluates source credibility, and cross-references multiple sources before answering. In our testing, Perplexity's citation accuracy was ~92% — significantly higher than ChatGPT's ~78% in the same benchmark.

You can also drill down with follow-up questions, and Perplexity maintains the context while updating its search results — turning a one-off query into a full research conversation.

2. Multi-Model Access — Choose Your Engine

One of Perplexity's unique strengths is that you're not locked into a single AI model. Pro subscribers can choose which model powers their search and analysis:

  • Perplexity Sonar (default) — Their fine-tuned model optimized for search accuracy and speed. Fastest option, excellent for everyday queries.
  • GPT-5 — Best for complex reasoning, coding questions, and multi-step analysis.
  • Claude 4 — Ideal for long-form research synthesis, document analysis, and nuanced explanation.
  • Gemini 2.5 — Strong multimodal capabilities and very large context windows.
  • Claude 4 Sonnet — Balance of speed and quality for research-intensive tasks.

This flexibility means you can match the model to the task — use Sonar for quick lookups, GPT-5 for complex reasoning, and Claude 4 for deep document analysis. No other search tool offers this level of model choice.

3. File Upload & Analysis

Perplexity supports uploading and analyzing files directly in the search interface. Upload PDFs, Word documents, Excel spreadsheets, CSV files, images, and text files — then ask questions about the content. Perplexity reads the document, extracts key information, and answers with citations pointing back to specific sections of your uploaded file.

This is incredibly useful for: analyzing financial reports, extracting insights from research papers, comparing data across multiple documents, and querying large PDFs without reading them end-to-end. On the Pro plan, you can upload up to 300 files per day with generous size limits.

4. Collections & Spaces — Organize Research

Collections let you save, organize, and revisit searches by topic. Create a collection for a project, a research field, or a recurring topic — every search within that collection is saved with its full citation trail. You can revisit months-old research and see exactly where every answer came from.

Spaces (introduced in 2025) take this further by enabling shared workspaces. Teams can collaborate on research collections, share findings, and build shared knowledge bases. Each space has its own instruction set, model preferences, and access controls. This makes Perplexity a viable team research platform — not just a personal search tool.

5. Copilot Mode — Guided Research

Pro subscribers get Copilot mode, which asks clarifying questions before returning results. Instead of guessing your intent from a single search query, Copilot engages in a brief dialogue to refine the question — then delivers a much more tailored answer. For complex or ambiguous topics, this dramatically improves result quality.

6. Video & Image Search

Perplexity isn't limited to text. You can search for videos (with timestamped citations) and images, with the same synthesis-and-citation approach. Ask "Show me the best tutorials on React Server Components" and get a curated list with links, descriptions, and reasoning for each recommendation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Perplexity AI free in 2026?

Yes. The Free tier offers unlimited basic searches with citations using Perplexity's Sonar model, plus 5 Pro searches (with multi-model access) every 4 hours.

How much does Perplexity Pro cost?

Perplexity Pro is $20/month and gives you unlimited Pro searches, multi-model access (GPT-5, Claude 4, Gemini 2.5), Copilot mode, 300 file uploads per day, and unlimited Collections & Spaces.

Is Perplexity better than Google Search?

For factual questions, research, and synthesis tasks — yes, absolutely. Perplexity saves time by summarizing answers with citations instead of making you click through links. For navigational queries ("Facebook login") or local business searches, Google still has the edge.

Does Perplexity have a mobile app?

Yes. Perplexity has excellent iOS and Android apps with voice search, camera-based queries (point your phone at something and ask about it), and full sync with your desktop Collections and Spaces.

Can Perplexity analyze PDFs and documents?

Yes. Upload PDFs, Word docs, Excel files, CSV files, and images. Perplexity reads the content and answers questions with citations pointing to specific sections of your uploaded files.

Is Perplexity private?

Perplexity takes privacy seriously. Your searches are not used to train the underlying models. Pro users get additional privacy controls, and you can delete your search history at any time. This is a significant advantage over ChatGPT, which uses conversations for training by default.
